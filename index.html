About National Day

UAE National Day marks the union of the seven emirates and the founding of the United Arab Emirates on 2 December 1971. It is a national celebration of unity, culture, and the country's rapid progress.

Short history

On 2 December 1971, six emirates united (Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al-Quwain and Fujairah); Ras Al Khaimah joined on 10 February 1972. The federation was formed under the leadership of the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan.

Why the colors?

The UAE flag colors each have meaning: green (fertility, growth), white (peace), black (strength of mind), and red (sacrifice and unity).

How people celebrate

Celebrations include parades, fireworks, cultural performances, light shows on landmarks, family gatherings, and displays of the flag. Many cities light up landmarks and public spaces in the flag colors.
